Lowell House Tutor, Harvard University

I spent two years living with Harvard undergraduate students. As a Lowell House Tutor I was part of the richness of the undergraduate life at Harvard. I academically advised and mentored students. I wrote recommendation letters. I organized events for this community. My job required me to be sensitive to the diverse needs and lifestyles of students, flexible regarding time demands, knowledgeable about support services available to students. Some of the projects I've been a part of:

    Undergraduate Sophomore Adviser, Fall 2011, 2012

    Academically advised sophomores in course choice and deciding on the concentration they would focus on during college.

    Lowell House Speeches, 2012, 2013

    The Speeches is a project that encourages students to speak publicly about meaningful stories for them and their student community. The resulting speeches are often deep, stricking and of personal nature. I co-organized the event, coordinated and advised student speakers, prepared audio setup, recordings, video editing and publishing.

Middle East Education Through Technology

MEET is a peace making program that uses technology as a tool to bring together Israeli and Palestinian students, two ethnic groups currently in conflict. I was a technical Instructor for the MEET program during Summer 2011. I taught Israeli and Palestinian high school students (Jerusalem and MIT). I prepared curriculum for 7 weeks of lecture and lab work in Java, Servlets, Databases, Javascript. I held lectures, labs and managed social events for the students.
