2013

Local Community News Creation

We explore a novel approach for producing news reports of geographic-specific events using locative crowdsourcing. We worked with local bloggers to identify people's needs and abilities to produce local stories. This work explores: the identification of basic templates for event reports, the task decomposition involved in creating them, the design of a system that automates the coordination of on-demand workforce to execute and produce said event reports. We evaluate the system by reporting on several events in different U.S. metropolitan areas.

Communicating Model Based Systems Engineering Information through Data Visualization

In the realm of systems engineering, data is organized across a multitude of tables that rank components in all kinds of dimensions. These tables are complicated enough as individual hierarchies of information and need to communicate crucial dependecies that span across them. In a design team of 3 people we did user research, prototyped and built an interactive data visualization tool. This tool supports engineers with different needs and background in assesing rapidly what changes in the system, to get cross system awareness and to explore the data.

Summer 2013, Jet Propulsion Lab, NASA

2012

Persuasive Technologies for Search Interfaces

We designed interfaces for search that encourage people to type longer queries and increase the diversity of documents seen in a search session. Results suggest that our interface manipulations may be effective for eliciting longer queries, but the effect is compromised when instructions to create longer queries are given. Our work also shows how the design of a search preview widget can lead users to engage with more diverse documents.

News and Participatory Media

Ehancing news reporting through technology. Prototyped a set of news tools focused on finding information from events that are under represented in the media, identifying voices in social media that are reporting on events. Used crowdsourcing on Mechanical Turk, regional blogs such as global voices, and social media platfors such as Twitter and Weibo.

More...

Finding information about what is being reported in a different country, where there is limited traditional media coverage and information on current events is hard to find.

Challenge: Traditional search using keywords tends to be successful in identifying information that is not trending in the social network.
Solution: We identified twitter hadles of people who are frequently talking about events in a foreign country. We used international blogs where people report on what is happening in their country through social media (globalvoices.com). We extracted the twitter accounts from blogs to seed the creation of twitter lists with people who are likely to speak about what is happening in a country at the moment. Slides and blog post.

Reporting on a story using crowdsourcing.

Challenge: Small news sources are not easy to identify online, especially when nobody quotes them, or when they are in a foreign language.
Solution: We used crowdsourcing to find news articles that did not come up in traditional search queries online or in social media. We used Amazon Mechanical Turk, a crowdsourcing platform to find people in India who could identify articles written in Hindi written on the topic of interest. This provided us with links to artiles in Hindi that did not show up in the original search. These articles provided additional information to the mainstream stories. The result was an online post reporting on a drowning incident in India.

Other projects included - using the Weibo api to identify folowers of users who get censored on Weibo, creating timelines to report on events, using link analysis to determine the story line of an event.

2008

Representing Mathematical Knowledge in a Web-Scalable Module System

A new method to organize mathematical knowledge is through representing mathematical theories in a computer system. There are several languages that can organize mathematical knowledge. The work is focused on the MMT language that provides a web scalable module system for mathematical theories. In MMT the theories are organized hierarchically which allows the representation of symbols and morphisms between theories. Model categories can be represented in MMT through theories. We propose an extension of MMT that will also allow the representation of functors through Î»- expressions abstracting over morphisms. We show the applicability of representing these functors. We then extended the grammar rules to allow the representation of the new structures. We finally added and adjusted the inference rules of the system according to the new structure of the grammar.
Fall 2008, Jacobs University Bremen, Knowledge Adaptation and Reasoning for Content Group, Undergraduate Research , Bachelor Thesis, Slides Logics

Second Opinion, a Collaborative Online Game for Medical Diagnosis

This work aims to develop an online intelligent diagnosis system, which will cooperate with human collaborators to improve its machine intelligence to provide fast and accurate diagnosis. We have developed an online interface so that a user can interact with the system as either a diagnoser or a diagnosee. As a diagnosee, the user can interact with the system to provide their symptoms. The system will use its intelligence to plan a strategy of questioning and finally provide a possible diagnosis. The system encourages the user to compete for points. Using interaction with human collaborators, we have developed a machine learning algorithm so that the system can automatically improve its questioning strategy and provide faster and more reliable diagnosis. When diagnosers provide a diagnostic, the system decides on the final diagnostic by consensus.
Summer 2008, Research Intern, UC Berkeley, Automation Sciences Lab , Full Report, Poster Machine Learning Collaborative Games Web Development

2007

Personal Environmental Impact Report

The project proposes and demonstrates how easily gathered location time series data can be used as an index into geospatial models to infer personal environmental impact and exposure. It focuses on three areas of interaction between individuals and the environment: transportation mode choice, overall carbon footprint, and opportunities for healthy eating. This class of applications represents a novel use of mobile systems, web-based mapping, and geospatial data and services. They pose interesting technical challenges and require multidisciplinary research.
